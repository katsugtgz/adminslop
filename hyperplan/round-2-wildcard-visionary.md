# Round 2 — Wildcard Visionary cross-attacks

### vs contrarian-minimalist
Fatal flaw — CM amputates before understanding buyer psychology. Calling 28 modules "bloated menu theater" ignores that Indonesian school admins often buy reassurance: Dinas-looking completeness, not elegant IA.
Unsupported claim — "46% is duplicate UI for the same 3 jobs." He gives no workflow evidence, no teacher interviews, no usage telemetry. Just vibes with a machete.
What they missed — compliance theater is sometimes the product. A "static docs site" for Panduan Kurikulum may be rational technically, but politically stupid if principals want one branded place to show Kurikulum Merdeka readiness.
One concession — "11 AI modules are the same feature wearing 11 masks" is correct. Separate AI UIs will multiply prompt, validation, and print bugs.

### vs quality-maximalist
Fatal flaw — QM designs like the first customer is a regulator, not a school. "No signature → no document → no export" will create rage at 23:40 WIB before inspection day.
Unsupported claim — "Byte-hash per combo" for print assumes deterministic browser/PDF output across Chrome/Safari/Firefox and OS fonts. That is fantasy unless frozen rendering environments exist.
What they missed — adoption risk. Field encryption, audit logs, provenance: fine. But no path for WhatsApp-native support, low-connectivity recovery, or teacher panic workflows. Quality that blocks survival gets bypassed.
One concession — UU PDP exposure is real. NIK, BPJS, minors, face images, medical history mean casual Firebase-client CRUD is malpractice.

### vs arch-logician
Fatal flaw — AL answers database purity while dodging product uncertainty. "RLS on shared schema" may be right, but it consumes oxygen before validating whether parent/Dinas/Yayasan are primary users.
Unsupported claim — "no school is GB-scale" is asserted without evidence. Photos, face images, PDFs, audit logs, generated docs, and attendance events can get ugly across districts.
What they missed — offline conflict semantics. `SET LOCAL app.tenant_id` does nothing for two teachers editing nilai offline, then syncing contradictory semester data after finalization lock.
One concession — the M:N question is critical. "teachers commonly teach at 2 schools; Yayasan chains common" is the rare architecture point that changes product surface and monetization.

### vs depth-researcher
Fatal flaw — DR confuses popularity with fit. Stars and starter kits do not prove suitability for Indonesian offline schools, A4-heavy bureaucracy, or Dinas procurement.
Unsupported claim — "WINNER: Next.js 15 + Supabase + Drizzle" rests on GitHub stars and feature checklists. No benchmark for offline PWA sync, print determinism, RLS ergonomics under school switching, or cheap AI queues.
What they missed — vendor and procurement risk. Supastarter "$299" is irrelevant if the rewrite inherits starter assumptions that fight multi-school roles, custom Dinas reports, or on-prem-ish anxieties.
One concession — "NO open machine-readable source exists" for Kurikulum Merdeka data is valuable. Hand-curated CP/TP/ATP seed data is a real moat if maintained.
